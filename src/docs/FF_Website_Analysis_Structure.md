# **2\. The Core Problem, Stated Plainly**

Three things came through clearly from the brief and the live audit:

1. **Very few customers find the website organically today.** Your intake data shows most patients come from word of mouth, then from referrals (specifically from Dr. Pemberton and other providers/partners).

| New patient source | Count | Share | Channel |
| :---- | ----- | ----- | :---- |
| Patient referral | 290 | **46.6%** | Word of mouth |
| Dr. Pemberton (OB referral) | 136 | **21.9%** | Provider referral |
| Google Search | 37 | **5.9%** | **Online — the site** |
| Provider & partner referrals (Ocean Dental, Haven, lactation, etc.) | \~80 | \~13% | Care network |
| Online directories & social | \~30 | \~5% | Online — not the site |

Over 80% of new patients are ***warm*** (sent by a friend). This means the website is failing at acquiring ***cold*** patients. This may be affecting warm patients as well, who go to the current website and may be turned off.  Right now the first impression of Flourishing Families feels  generic and doesn’t reflect the speciality you have.

2. **The site sells services, not outcomes.** This isn’t a “Groupon quick-fix visit.” The stressed 38-year-old mom in Cranford wants to feel like herself again and the site needs to connect her problem to your solution.

3. **New and higher-value services are invisible.** In our call we pointed out the need for outcome-based service products. 

**Bottom line: the rebuild’s job is to convert warm referrals reliably and to make your highest-value programs findable and sellable.** Every structural decision below serves those two goals.

# **3\. Current Site Audit**

## **3.1  Current Structure**

The live navigation is organized by category:

* **Chiropractic** → Pregnancy \+ Pediatric, Sports \+ Extremity Care, Animal Chiropractic, Techniques (Gonstead, Non-Force)

* **Acupuncture**

* **Coaching** → Coaching, Our Store

* **Functional Nutrition**

## **3.2  What’s Working — Keep These**

* **Genuine social proof.** The testimonials are specific and warm (“they support you like family,” “best chiropractor in 20 years”). These are an asset that we’ll feature prominently.

* **Credibility markers.** “110K+ adjustments given” and “practicing since 2011” are strong trust signals currently buried mid-page.

* **A real brand voice exists.** “If you get your tires rotated more than your nervous system gets regulated…” is memorable and distinctive. 

* **Booking is already wired.** Sked (portal.sked.life) handles new-patient booking for both chiropractic and acupuncture. 

## **3.3  What’s Holding the Site Back**

| Issue | Why it matters / what it costs |
| :---- | :---- |
| **No outcome-based positioning** | Pages list techniques without a clear goal/transformation. Visitors can’t self-identify (“that’s me, that’s my problem”), so they bounce or call without context. |
| **Missing program & service pages** | No fertility, pregnancy-program, or other outcome-based service page.  |
| **No “Start here” path** | A first-time visitor faces a menu of clinical terms with no guided next step. Short and long term expectations are not set, so price-shoppers and committed patients are treated identically. |
| **Weak local SEO foundation** | Google Business and Yelp listings use mismatched names. This harms local ranking signals. No location-specific service pages to capture “prenatal chiropractor near Cranford”-type searches. |
| **Platform fragility** | The recent homepage 404 traced to WordPress \+ NitroPack cache conflicts. The current site is fragile and difficult to maintain. |
| **Stale / orphaned content** | Discontinued group-coaching page still live.. |

# **4\. Strategic Direction for the Structure**

## **4.1  Reposition from “chiropractic office” to “family wellness hub”**

We anchor the new structure around the journey the brief identifies as the biggest opportunity: **preconception → pregnancy → postpartum → pediatric → whole-family**. Pregnancy leads because, in your words, it has “a clear timeline”, “they’re encouraged by a medical team they trust”.

## **4.2  Build the structure around one ideal patient**

The ideal patient is a **stressed professional mom, 30–45, in Westfield / Scotch Plains / Cranford / Clark**, with school-aged kids and her own disposable income. She has aches she’s been told are “just aging,” she doesn’t feel old, and she wants to run / do yoga / feel like “herself again”. She often arrives by referral and trusts her doula, OB, or midwife. Every top-level page should let her, (or the pregnant version of her)  instantly recognize herself.

## **4.3  Make programs first-class citizens**

The three signature offers from the Growth Plan — **Fertility / Preconception Journey, Pregnancy Care Program, and the Family Wellness Membership** — each get a dedicated, conversion-focused landing page. These are where outcome-based selling, value stacks, guarantees, and (later) direct purchase via Stax/ThriveCart will live. The architecture must let you add and retire programs yourself without a developer.

* **“Coaching / Our Store” retired** — the discontinued page is gone; nutrition becomes a service and a program component.

* **“Animal Chiropractic” moved or retired** — it dilutes the family-wellness focus. Recommend a single mention under Services or removing it; your call (see Section 6).

# **6\. Key Page Intent (Conversion Logic)**

For the highest-value pages, what each must accomplish. This becomes the brief for wireframes.

| Page | Visitor mindset | Page must do |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Home** | “My friend / doula sent me here. Are these my people?” | Reflect her back in one line; name the journeys (fertility/pregnancy/family); show proof (thousands of adjustments, since 2011, testimonials); one clear “Start Here” and one “Book.” |
| **Pregnancy Care Program** | “I’m pregnant, anxious, and was told to come. Is this safe and worth it?” | Lead with the transformation (comfortable pregnancy, confident birth); show the journey/timeline; address safety (Webster cert) and cost up front; one strong CTA. |
| **Fertility & Preconception** | “I’ve tried things. I’m tired of false hope. Can this actually help?” | Empathy first; honest framing (we optimize the body, never promise a result); the roadmap call as a low-risk entry; acupuncture-led credibility (Dr. Alexandra). |
| **Family Wellness Membership** | “We love it here. Is there a simpler way to bring the whole family?” | Belonging over transaction; value vs. à-la-carte; convenience (one family visit block); easy pause; clear monthly price. |
| **Start Here / New Patient** | “What do I actually do, what does it cost, how long is this?” | Set the 6–12 month expectation honestly. Answer insurance/pricing and describe the first visit. Filter for fit so calls are warmer. |
| **About / Meet the Doctors** | “Who will I actually see? Are they real, are they good?” | Tell the “you \+ a doctor, every time — no PAs, no students” story. credentials in plain english. |
