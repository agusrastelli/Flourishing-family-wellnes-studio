# **3\. The Creative Direction**

Three decisions define the feel of the whole site.

## **3.1  The feeling: warm, calm, quietly elevated**

Your ideal patient: the stressed professional mom told “there’s nothing to be done”. They should feel **reassured, hopeful, and cared for** the moment she lands. **Settled;** your brand palette already supports this beautifully with the warm cream and deep plum feel inviting.

## **3.2  The aesthetic: natural & editorial, not medical & corporate**

Think elevated wellness brand or thoughtful lifestyle magazine, not a medical-practice template. Large imagery of **real moments** (you and your team working with real patients), short evocative headlines, and generous space around every idea. 

## **3.3  The voice: a doctor talking to you, one-on-one**

Your biggest differentiator, in your words: “it’s a doctor’s office where you are one-on-one with a doctor. No PAs, no nurses, no students.” The writing should sound exactly like that. Warm, direct, a little wry (your “tires rotated more than your nervous system regulated” line is perfect). Avoids sounding salesy or clinical.

## **4.2  Proposed evolution: how the palette works on the web**

A full brand palette and a **functional web palette** aren’t the same thing. A website needs a clear hierarchy: one or two colors that mean “action,” a calm neutral base, and accessible text contrast. Eight equal colors would compete and feel busy. Here’s how we propose assigning roles — nothing new is invented; we’re prioritizing what you already own.

| Web role | Color | Why |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Primary action / CTA** | **Amber**  \#eba040 | Warmest, most energetic color — draws the eye to “Book.” Pair with plum or teal text for legibility (amber alone fails contrast for text). |
| **Primary brand / headers** | **Deep Plum**  \#483e5e | The sophisticated anchor. Headlines, nav, key sections. This is what makes the brand feel premium and adult, not generic-wellness. |
| **Background base** | **Cream**  \#fcf0dd | Warm, calm canvas — replaces clinical white everywhere. |
| **Secondary accent** | **Sage Teal**  \#608f84 | Natural, balancing. Icons, supporting tags, the “natural health” cue. Keeps warmth from tipping into too-sweet. |
| **Body text** | **Deep Teal**  \#194247 | Near-black with warmth — softer and more on-brand than pure black, and passes accessibility contrast on cream. |
| **Soft section fills** | **Lavender / Mauve**  \#e2dce2 / \#6e5770 | Alternating section backgrounds and gradients for gentle rhythm, exactly as your guidelines already use them. |

**The one honest recommendation:** the palette leans warm-to-sweet (amber, gold, lavender). Leaning on **deep plum and teal as the structural colors** — and using amber as a precise accent rather than everywhere — is what will make the site feel **expensive and credible** rather than soft. This is a usage recommendation, not a change to your colors.

# **6\. Layout Principles**

The rules every page follows, so the whole site feels like one intentional thing:

* **One idea per section.** Each scroll stop makes a single point: a headline, a short line, an image. No crowding.

* **Imagery-led, real people.** Large warm photos of you, your team, and real families. Zero stock lab coats or PT/massage imagery.

* **A persistent, obvious “Book” action.** The path to Sked is always one tap away, so the visitor always knows what to do.

* **Proof woven throughout.** Testimonials, “thousands of adjustments,” “since 2011,” and credentials appear at decision moments –\> not buried on one page.

* **Mobile-first.** Your referred patients look you up on their phones first. Designed for small screens, then scaled up.

* **Generous whitespace as a feature.** Space is what makes it feel calm and premium.
